Accountability: About This Data Set

This is a federal dataset published by Center for Disease Control and Prevention. This dataset presents the age-adjusted death rates for the 10 leading causes of death in the United States beginning in 1999. Data are based on information from all resident death certificates filed in the 50 states and the District of Columbia using demographic and medical characteristics. Age-adjusted death rates (per 100,000 population) are based on the 2000 U.S. standard population.

Where did this data come from?

The dataset was found on Data.gov, the home of the U.S. government's open data. This dataset was under the category of Centers for Disease Control and Prevention, which is part of U.S. Department of Health & Human Services

Who has access to it?

This dataset is intended for public access and use. The data is in CSV, RDF, JSON, and XSL format.
